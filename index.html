paintings

complementary (sunflowers and lavenders)

acrylic on canvas, 2025

I tend to think about how objects like flowers, colours, or even small gestures can carry meaning between people. Lavenders are something I've always adored, and the sunflowers are tied to someone important to me. The red background felt like the right way to hold both things together. This was also one of my first times working in an impressionist style, which is really about letting go of details and focusing more on how something feels than how it looks. In other words, making artistic suggestions of what is seen.

cracked (fruit bowl)

fresco attached to wood, 2023

This piece explores the theme of fragility and impermanence. The cracked surface of the fruit bowl serves as a metaphor for the passage of time and the inevitability of change. I wanted to capture the beauty in these imperfections, reminding us that even in our brokenness, there is still value and meaning.

last light

acrylic on canvas, 2024

I made this when I was learning to let go of old habits or outgrown friendships. Endings don't always have to be dramatic or climatic. They can be silent and sort of peaceful? This was my way of being at peace with it.

lotus

acrylic on canvas, 2022

The lotus is one of my favourite flowers and is a symbol of resilience for me. It grows in muddy water but still blooms beautifully above the surface. I painted this during a time when I needed to remind myself that growth can happen even in difficult circumstances. The soft colors and gentle ripples are meant to evoke a sense of quiet strength and hope.

soft bloom

oil on canvas, 2024

This piece was done in the spring, usually my favourite season. It's when things tend to start over to me, when everything, including nature, wakes up. But that year, I didn't feel any of that. I felt stuck, even as the world around me bloomed. I was unsure if the "reset" would ever happen. This painting was done solely on that feeling.

drawings

bermuda in passing

charcoal on paper, 2023

I made this while on a tour of the island, my sketchbook balanced on my lap, and charcoal smudging under my fingers as the island moved past. It wasn't a planned piece, more like a passing moment I felt the need to capture before it disappeared.

identity stripes

chalk pastel on paper, 2023

This piece started with a pronunciation debate: "zeh-bruh" vs "zee-bruh." It reminded me of how much I had to relearn after moving countries. Zebra stripes blur into each other, and so do the different parts of my identity.

panda

charcoal on paper, 2025

This was a fun piece to do. I wanted to capture the panda's gentle nature with my version of impressionism without focusing on too much detail, keeping it soft and loose.

pears study

charcoal on paper, 2023

A simple study of two pears. I wanted to practice soft shading and gentle highlights, keeping the composition quiet and understated.

pineapple study

charcoal on paper, 2023

retina

charcoal on paper, 2025

This is a charcoal drawing of the eye of someone I care deeply about. I focused on capturing the details like the lashes, the light in the iris—because those are the things that stayed with me. The rest of the piece is looser, more impressionistic, which felt right.

sundae in a cup?

colour pencil on paper, 2023

A study of glass and reflections. I wanted to practice capturing transparency and the way light bends through curved surfaces. I'm not quite sure what's in the glass cup but there were so many elements which were fun to draw! I added a 'messy' pencil background to make the main subject stand out.

the observer

charcoal on paper, 2023

This piece reflects the time when I first moved to America and felt the need to blend in. I became quiet for the first few years, intentionally watching like a camera capturing moments. I studied how people spoke, moved, and interacted, then replayed those moments in my head at home, practicing how to fit in. This drawing is a tribute to that version of me, the one who observed to adapt, to belong.

what remains

charcoal on paper, 2024

Honestly, I chose the skull as a subject partly because I really wanted to push my rendering skills; getting those technical details and the way light and shadow play across bone is something I very much enjoy. But the more time I spent on it, the more I couldn't help but notice how I felt towards it. What remains is a reminder of how we all have a shared foundation no matter how different we may appear on the outside. We all return to this fundamental structure at the end.

ascension

charcoal on paper, 2025

I made this while thinking about what it feels like to stop shrinking yourself. The upward tilt wasn't planned, but once it was there, it felt honest. The neck is exposed, which makes the figure vulnerable, but also open. It feels like a quiet decision to exist fully, without lowering yourself or looking away.

bearing

charcoal on paper, 2025

This piece came from observing how much can be felt without seeing someone's face. The back holds tension in a different way. It made me think about all the versions of myself that learned to be smaller, quieter, more careful. This figure isn't hiding, but it isn't performing either. It's just existing, and that feels important.

about

Hi, my name is Abiola. I'm a visual artist & this is my digital art portfolio! My pieces tend to explore themes like perception, imperfection, and identity. I usually work in charcoal and acrylics. A lot of my work comes from observation. After moving to the U.S., I became very aware of how I existed in spaces; sometimes visible, sometimes unnoticed. Drawing became a way for me to slow down and understand those moments. I'm especially drawn to the human form, because posture, light, and stillness can reveal things that words often cannot.
